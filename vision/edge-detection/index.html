Conclusion

While the Marr-Hildreth edge detector can pick up many discrete pixels in the image, many of these are false edges, and just contribute noise to the result. The Canny edge detector is much better at discerning true edges by considering the gradient of each pixel and whether it is a local maximum. Its hysteresis allows weak edges to connect strong edges that might not otherwise be connected. Marr-Hildreth illustrates what it looks like when even the real edges are not completely connected.
