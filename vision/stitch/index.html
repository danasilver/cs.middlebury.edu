Battell

The Battell images were taken from different degrees of rotation and slightly different angles. The small difference in angle worked well with the affine transformation used by our stitcher. The two Battell images stay within the 6 degrees of freedom allowed by the affine transform. The images only differ in x and y translation and rotation. The change in angle isn't noticable between the images.

Bookshelf

The two bookshelf images were taken from different angles. The change in perspective made it difficult for the matcher to find matches (it could only find 3) and for the affine transform to properly stitch one image onto the other. It needed more than the 6 degrees of freedom allowed by an affine transform. In an affine transform parallel lines must stay parallel. While the bottom left corner looks properly aligned, the right edge needs to skew so pixels are placed farther apart higher up the image. This would violate parallel lines staying parallel.
